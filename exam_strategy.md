# HPCL General Aptitude: Strategic Breakdown & Exam Insights

This document provides a comprehensive analysis and strategic guide for the General Aptitude section (Paper I) of the HPCL Engineer/Officer examination.

---

## 📋 Exam Overview
*   **Total Questions:** 85 Multiple-Choice Questions (MCQs).
*   **Total Duration:** 150 Minutes (Combined with 85 technical questions).
*   **Marking Scheme:** **No Negative Marking**. Candidates are encouraged to attempt all 170 questions.
*   **Core Subjects:** Quantitative Aptitude, Intellectual Potential Test (Reasoning), and English Language.

---

## 1. Quantitative Aptitude (Approx. 34 Questions)
*   **Pattern:** High weightage on **Arithmetic** over Advanced Math. 
*   **Key Topics:**
    *   **Arithmetic:** Percentage, Ratio & Proportion, SI & CI, Mixture & Alligation, Time & Work, Time, Speed & Distance, Averages, Quadratic Equations, HCF & LCM.
    *   **Advanced/Misc:** Mensuration (Min 2 questions), Trigonometry (1-2), Geometry (1-2), Statistics (Mean, Median, Mode).
*   **Difficulty:** Easy to Moderate (Moderate due to lengthy calculations).
*   **Strategic Insights:**
    *   **Ratio Method:** Avoid the 'X' method for Ages, Mixture, and Boats/Streams. Use ratios or option-checking.
    *   **Calculation Speed:** Memorize squares up to 30 and cubes up to 20.
    *   **Prioritize:** Solve 10-15 second arithmetic questions first to build momentum.

---

## 2. Intellectual Potential Test / Reasoning (Approx. 34 Questions)
*   **Core Topics:** Coding-Decoding, Seating Arrangement, Series, Direction Sense, Syllogism, Blood Relation, Analogy.
*   **Unique Pattern: Depiction & Interpretation (Guaranteed ~4 Questions)**
    *   **Type 1 (Alphanumeric Series):** Counting sequences (e.g., Symbol-Letter-Number).
    *   **Type 2 (3-Digit Numbers):** Operations on digits of multiple 3-digit numbers.
    *   **Type 3 (Alphabet Shifting):** Shifting vowels/consonants and checking result properties.
*   **Strategic Insights:**
    *   **Alphanumeric Hack:** Skip the 3-4 line instruction text. Go straight to the condition (e.g., Symbol-Symbol-Letter) and scan the series immediately.

---

## 3. English Language (17 Questions)
*   **Core Topics:** Reading Comprehension (RC), Jumbled Sentences, Error Spotting, Fill in the Blanks, Vocabulary.
*   **Difficulty:** Easy to Moderate.
*   **Strategic Insights:**
    *   **Reading Comprehension (4-5 Qs):** Read the questions **before** the passage. Skim for keywords. Find the central theme in the first or last lines.
    *   **Jumbled Sentences (2-3 Qs):** Identify the **Noun (Subject)** first. Sentences starting with Pronouns or transitional words ("Therefore", "However") are rarely the opening sentence.
    *   **Grammar Focus:** Subject-Verb Agreement (e.g., "A number of..." vs "The number of...") and Tenses (Conditionals).
